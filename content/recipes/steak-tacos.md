## Ingredients

- ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves
- 3 scallions, chopped coarse
- 3 garlic cloves, chopped coarse
- 1 jalapeño chile, stemmed
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- 6 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 (1¼ to 1¾-pound) flank steak, trimmed
- ½ teaspoon table salt
- ½ teaspoon sugar
- ½ teaspoon pepper
- 12 (6-inch) corn tortillas, warmed
- Lime wedges

## Instructions

1. Throw the cilantro, scallions, garlic, jalapeño, and cumin into a food processor and pulse until roughly chopped (10–12 pulses). Pour in ¼ cup oil and run until smooth, about 15 seconds. Scoop out 2 tablespoons of the herb paste into a separate bowl and stir in the lime juice — that's your serving sauce.
2. Cut the steak along the grain into 4 pieces. Stab each piece all over with a fork (10–12 times per side) — this helps the marinade penetrate.
3. Season the steaks with salt, then smear the remaining herb paste over all surfaces. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
4. [?] Remaining steps on page 167 — likely sear or grill steaks, slice against grain, serve in tortillas with reserved herb sauce and lime wedges.
