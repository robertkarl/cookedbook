## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons water [?]
- 1 (1½-pound) [?] flank steak, trimmed
- 4 shallots, sliced thin
- 1 seedless English cucumber, sliced ¼-inch thick on bias [?]
- 1½ cups fresh mint leaves, torn
- 1½ cups fresh cilantro leaves
- 1 Thai chile, stemmed, seeded, and sliced thin into rounds

## Dressing

- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1 tablespoon white rice powder [?]
- 3 tablespoons lime juice

## Instructions

1. Warm the paprika and cayenne in a small dry skillet over medium heat, shaking the pan, until the spices smell toasty (about a minute). Dump them into a bowl. Crank the heat to medium-high, add the rice to the same skillet, and stir constantly until it turns deep golden brown, around 5 minutes. Let the rice cool a few minutes, then grind it to a fine powder in a spice grinder, mini food processor, or mortar and pestle [?]. You're aiming for about 1 tablespoon of rice powder.
2. Build the dressing: whisk together the lime juice, fish sauce, water, sugar, and ¼ teaspoon of the toasted rice powder in a large bowl. Set it aside.
3. **Charcoal grill:** Open all vents. Light a full chimney of briquettes. When the top coals are ashed over, bank them to one side [?]. Set the grate, cover, and let it heat for 5 minutes. **Gas grill:** Crank all burners to high [?].
4. Season the steak with salt and pepper. Grill over direct high heat until you get good char and the center reads 130–135°F (medium-rare), roughly 5 minutes per side [?]. Rest 5 minutes, slice thin against the grain, then cut the slices into bite-size pieces.
5. Toss the sliced steak into the dressing bowl along with the shallots, cucumber, mint, cilantro, and chile. Give it a good toss, hit it with the remaining rice powder, and serve right away.

## Notes

- Toasted rice powder (khao khua) is the signature ingredient — provides nutty crunch. Can find it pre-made at Asian markets.
