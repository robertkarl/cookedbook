## Ingredients

- 1 (2-pound) flank steak, trimmed
- 8 [?] garlic cloves, sliced thin
- 10 [?] giardiniera pieces, diced [?]
- ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- ⅓ cup chopped fresh basil, divided
- 1 tablespoon red pepper flakes [?]
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Pepper
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes

## Instructions

1. Lay the flank steak on a cutting board oriented so the grain runs horizontally. Cover with plastic wrap and pound it out to a uniform ½-inch thickness [?] — you want even cooking when it braises.
2. Scatter the garlic slices, giardiniera, Parmesan, half the basil, red pepper flakes, and oregano across the surface of the steak. Hit it with some pepper.
3. Starting from the long edge closest to you, roll the steak up tightly into a log. Tie it off with kitchen twine every inch or so. Season the outside with salt and pepper.
4. Heat oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high. Brown the braciole on all sides, rotating it every couple minutes, until you get good color all around — about 6–8 minutes [?] total. Pull it out and set aside.
5. Drop the onion into the pot and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add garlic [?] and cook a minute, then stir in the tomato paste and let it darken for another minute.
6. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and bring everything to a simmer. Nestle the braciole back in, cover, and let it braise gently — turning it once or twice — until fork-tender, roughly 1½ hours [?].
7. Pull the braciole out, snip off the twine, and slice into rounds. Spoon the sauce over pasta, lay the braciole rounds on top, and finish with the remaining basil.
