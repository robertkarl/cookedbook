## Ingredients

- 1 pound flank steak, trimmed and cut into 2 to 2½-inch strips
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1½ teaspoons baking soda [?]
- ½ cup oyster sauce
- ¼ cup water [?]
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons sugar [?]
- 1½ teaspoons coarsely ground pepper
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 1 red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and cut into strips [?]
- 1 green bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and cut into strips
- 6 scallions, white parts sliced thin on bias, green parts cut into 1-inch pieces
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger

## Instructions

1. Slice the steak along the grain into 2 to 2½-inch wide strips, then cut each strip across the grain into thin ¼-inch slices. Toss the sliced beef with 1 tablespoon soy sauce, the cornstarch, and baking soda in a bowl. Leave it alone at room temperature for 30 minutes — the baking soda does its tenderizing thing during this time.
2. Mix the sauce: whisk the oyster sauce, water [?], remaining soy sauce, sugar, and pepper together in a small bowl. Set aside.
3. Get a 12-inch nonstick skillet ripping hot over high heat with 1 tablespoon oil. Lay half the beef in a single layer and leave it undisturbed for a full minute to get good color. Flip and cook another minute until you see patchy browning. Pull the beef out.
4. Do the same with the second batch of beef.
5. Drop the last tablespoon of oil into the skillet. Toss in the bell pepper strips and scallion greens, stir-frying until they soften slightly [?], 2–3 minutes.
6. Push the veggies aside, add garlic and ginger, let them bloom for about 30 seconds. Dump the beef back in along with the sauce. Toss everything together until the sauce thickens and coats, roughly 30 seconds.
7. Serve over steamed white rice.

## Notes

- Baking soda tenderizes the beef — don't skip the 30-minute rest. Don't leave it longer than that either.
- Cut against the grain on the thin slices, with the grain on the long strips.
