## Ingredients

- 1 pound flank steak, trimmed
- 5 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce [?]
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce [?]
- 2 tablespoons ketchup
- 1½ tablespoons packed brown sugar
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- 1 pound fresh or dried lo mein noodles [?]
- 1 green bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and cut into strips [?]
- 1½ cups packed shredded cabbage [?]
- 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and sliced
- 6 scallions, white parts sliced thin on bias, green parts cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger

## Instructions

1. Slice the steak along the grain into 2 to 2½-inch wide strips, then cut each strip across the grain into thin ¼-inch slices. In a bowl, mix 1 tablespoon soy sauce [?], the cornstarch, and baking soda. Add the beef slices, toss to coat, and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.
2. Stir together the sauce: Worcestershire, remaining soy sauce, ketchup, and brown sugar in a small bowl.
3. Boil the noodles per the package directions. Drain, hit them with cold water to stop the cooking, then toss with 1 tablespoon of oil [?] so they don't clump.
4. Get a 12-inch nonstick screaming hot over high heat with 1 tablespoon oil. Spread the beef in a single layer and let it sit untouched for a full minute to build color. Stir briefly until you see patchy browning, about another minute. Pull the beef out.
5. Add the last of the oil to the skillet. Toss in the cabbage, bell pepper, and mushrooms — cook, stirring now and then, until they soften up, about 4 minutes. Add the scallion whites and ginger, stir for 30 seconds.
6. Add the noodles and sauce to the skillet. Toss everything together over heat until the noodles are hot and glossy with sauce, about 2 minutes.
7. Return the beef, add the scallion greens, give it one final toss, and serve immediately.
