## Adobo

- 1½ ounces dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 1 ounce dried pasilla chiles, stemmed and seeded
- ¼ cup salsa verde
- ¼ cup chicken broth
- ¼ cup tomato juice [?]
- ¼ cup brown sugar [?]
- 1½ teaspoons dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon table salt
- ¾ teaspoon pepper

## Flank Steak

- 1 (1¾ to 2-pound) [?] flank steak, trimmed and cut into 1¼-inch cubes [?]
- ½ teaspoon table salt
- Pepper
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 1 onion, chopped fine [?]
- 8 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 4 ounces queso fresco, crumbled (1 cup)
- ¾ cup coarsely chopped fresh cilantro
- 12 (8-inch) flour tortillas, warmed

## Instructions

1. Heat a dry skillet over medium and toast the chiles, flipping occasionally, until they smell warm and earthy (a couple minutes). Transfer to a bowl, cover with boiling water, and let them rehydrate until pliable [?].
2. Drain the chiles and throw them in a blender with the salsa verde, broth, tomato juice, sugar, oregano, salt, and pepper. Blitz until you have a smooth adobo sauce.
3. Dry the steak cubes thoroughly with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper. Get your oil ripping hot in a Dutch oven or heavy skillet over high heat, then brown the beef in batches — don't crowd the pan [?].
4. Pour in the adobo sauce, drop to a simmer, and let it go until the meat is tender and the sauce has reduced to a thick, clingy consistency [?].
5. Serve in warm tortillas, topped with crumbled queso fresco and a generous handful of cilantro.
