## Ingredients

- 1½ pounds small Yukon Gold potatoes, unpeeled, quartered
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 3 tablespoons red curry paste
- ¼ cup coconut milk [?]
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 2 teaspoons sugar [?]
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro leaves

## Flank Steak

- 1 (1½-pound) [?] flank steak, trimmed
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Toss the quartered potatoes with 2 tablespoons oil in a microwave-safe bowl. Cover and nuke until they're almost tender when poked with a knife, about 7 minutes [?]. This par-cooks them so they'll crisp up fast in the skillet.
2. Blot the steak dry and season generously with salt and pepper. Heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in a 12-inch nonstick over medium-high until it shimmers and just barely smokes. Sear the steak until deeply browned, 5–7 minutes per side — pull it at 130–135°F for medium-rare. Rest on a cutting board under a foil tent for 5 minutes [?].
3. In the same skillet (all those fond bits are flavor), fry the curry paste, ginger, and garlic until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add the par-cooked potatoes and let them get golden and crispy [?], roughly 3 minutes. Stir in the coconut milk, fish sauce, and sugar.
4. Slice the steak thin against the grain. Plate over the curried potatoes and scatter cilantro on top.

- Some ingredient amounts uncertain from photo — verify against book.
- Yukon Golds hold up well to the curry treatment. Don't substitute russets.
