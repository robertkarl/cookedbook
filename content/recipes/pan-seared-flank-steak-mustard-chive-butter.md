## Ingredients

- 1 (1½ to 1¾-pound) flank steak, trimmed
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon pepper
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
- 3 tablespoons chopped fresh chives, divided
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon zest plus 1 teaspoon juice
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

## Instructions

1. Set your oven to 225°F with a rack in the middle. Dry the steak thoroughly, then halve it lengthwise and cut each half crosswise — you'll end up with 4 smaller steaks.
2. Mix the salt, sugar, and pepper. Season all surfaces of each steak.
3. Set the steaks on a wire rack over a rimmed baking sheet. Slide them into the oven and let them slowly come up to 120°F internal — this takes 30–40 minutes depending on thickness. The low heat gives you edge-to-edge even doneness.
4. While the steaks bake, mash together the softened butter, 1 tablespoon of the chives, mustard, and lemon zest and juice. This is your compound butter.
5. When the steaks hit temp, get a 12-inch skillet screaming hot over medium-high with the oil. Sear the steaks, flipping every minute, until both sides develop a deep brown crust — about 4 minutes total. The sear is just for the crust; the oven already did the cooking.
6. Rest the steaks on a wire rack for 10 minutes.
7. Slice as thin as you can against the grain. Top each portion with compound butter and scatter the remaining chives over everything.

## Notes

- Variations exist with Garlic-Anchovy Butter and Spinach-Lime Butter (substitute the compound butter ingredients).
- Reverse sear gives you edge-to-edge even doneness on a thin cut — the oven does the cooking, the skillet is just for crust.
